Fragments of Caligo

Physical manifestations of the Formless One's influence. The Church wants them contained. The Cult wants them worshipped. The truth is worse than either.

Three Fragments exist in the known world. Each is a crystallized shard of Caligo's presence — not a piece of the entity itself, but an echo of the void it leaves behind wherever it passes. The Church calls them sacred enemy relics. The Cult calls them holy instruments. Both descriptions are wrong in the same way.

Largest Fragment of Caligo
Eye Form · Fragment · Legendary
Function
Dropped by the Cult Leader upon defeat. Completing the trio of Fragments is the central objective of the Mercenary's mission. Possessing it triggers the final decision node: deliver to the Church, or withhold.
Description
A dense, asymmetrical shard roughly the size of a fist, dark as obsidian but veined with light that does not come from any external source. The surface is warm — not uncomfortably so, but insistently. Looking directly at it for too long produces a sensation described by every carrier as recognition.
Lore
The Cult of Caligo has held this Fragment since the Church expelled its founding members. It was not stolen — it was carried out by a child who simply refused to leave it behind. The Cult built their entire theology around it: the Eye Form, they call it, the gaze of the Formless One rendered solid. The Church calls it a corruption node. The Librarian Scholar called it a mirror. He did not explain what it reflects.
⚠ MS +12 on acquisition. At MS > 75, carriers report hearing a voice that uses their original name — the one before the Church renamed them.
Flame Fragment of Caligo
Fire Form · Fragment · Legendary
Function
Found in the Tribe Area. Voluntarily surrendered by Pluvia, the Shaman, who has kept it protected from both the Church and Cult. Holding it in combat grants a passive aura that causes hesitation in Eldritch Horrors — they pause for 1.5 seconds before engaging. This advantage vanishes above MS 70.
Description
Translucent, amber-hued, shaped like a flame frozen mid-flicker. Unlike other Fragments, this one flickers visibly — a slow, rhythmic pulse that matches no known combustion pattern. It does not burn the holder's skin, but nearby parchment browns at the edges if left adjacent overnight.
Lore
The Tribe's elders have passed the Flame Fragment down through seventeen generations without ever fully understanding what they were guarding. Their oral tradition calls it the memory that refuses to cool. Pluvia's Foresight has told her, three times and in three separate visions, that if this Fragment reaches the Church, something ends that cannot be started again. She gives it away anyway. The visions showed that too.
⚠ The Tribe Elder's last words before the Shaman departs: "Bring her back when it is done. If you can." The qualifier is not accidental.
Void Fragment of Caligo
Shadow Form · Fragment · Legendary
Function
Located along the path toward Cultist Island from the Port City.
Description
Perfectly spherical, matte black, approximately the weight of a stone twice its size. No visible seam, no texture, no temperature. It absorbs ambient light in a radius of roughly three feet. Objects placed next to it cast no shadow because the Fragment absorbs shadow itself.
Lore
The Church's mission briefing does not mention this Fragment. It exists in their records under an administrative category titled Indirect Artifacts — Non-Priority Recovery. The record was amended fourteen years ago. Before the amendment, it was listed as Priority One — Do Not Approach Unaccompanied. The reason for the amendment is redacted. Father Calum's personal archive contains a single note: "The Void Fragment does not corrupt. It clarifies. That is the problem."
⚠ Unique: grants Caligo Perception passive. Trade-off — the world rendered clearly at all MS levels is not always preferable to the distorted version.

The Enlightenment Artifacts

Items born from the Formless One of Enlightenment — knowledge made tangible, or knowledge weaponized.

Book of Enlightenment
Special Book · Artifact · Legendary
Crafted from Ancient Paper → Book of Enlightenment
Function / Effect
The Book of Enlightenment carries the save effect — the only item in the game capable of preserving a character's current state.
Description
Bound in material that resembles gold leaf but is not — closer to compressed light, if that were a material. The pages resist tearing. The text within is not static: it changes between readings, reflecting the reader's current knowledge state back at them with additions the reader did not put there. No two characters read the same book. The words are always legible. The meaning is always just beyond comfortable comprehension.
Lore
The Formless One of Enlightenment is never named in the game's dialogue — only implied. Where Caligo is described as existing the way weather exists, Enlightenment is its inverse: a presence that does not corrupt but illuminates, and not always kindly. The Book is the only confirmed physical artifact of Enlightenment's influence. It was not created by scholars or the Church. The Ancient Paper from which it is crafted predates the Theocratic capital by four hundred years. The Librarian Scholar, in his extended dialogue, says only: "There are things that want to be known. The book is one of them." He does not explain further. He never explains further. That is the whole of what Enlightenment gives: the information, without the comfort of understanding it.
Notes
The Book of Enlightenment is the game's rarest crafted item. Its relationship to Caligo is oppositional but not adversarial — Caligo corrupts through absence of meaning; Enlightenment overwhelms through excess of it. Both result in the same risk: a mind that cannot hold what it has been given.
Ancient Paper
Material · Crafting Component · Rare
Used in Book of Enlightenment
Function / Effect
Primary crafting material for the Book of Enlightenment. Cannot be used in any other recipe. Found in the Records Archive of the Theocratic Capital — the document references "dual-purpose acquisition" of the Fragments, noting that the Cult's ritual use and the Church's containment protocols share more methodology than either party admits. This is the first evidence, available to any character who finds it, that the Church and Cult are two expressions of the same underlying design.
Description
Yellowed but intact despite its age. The text upon it is in three scripts, two of which no character in the game can read. The third is administrative Church Latin, and it is the clearest and most damning of the three. The paper does not age further once found — it simply holds its condition indefinitely, the way certain truths resist decay no matter how long they are suppressed.
Lore
The Formless One of Enlightenment did not create the Ancient Paper directly. What Enlightenment did was ensure that whoever needed to find it would find it — that the document would survive every fire, flood, and deliberate destruction the Church attempted. There are singed corners. There are water stains. There is a circular burn mark consistent with something very hot being placed on it and then removed. The text beneath the burn mark is perfectly legible. The Formless One of Enlightenment is patient in a way that has nothing to do with virtue and everything to do with certainty.

Complete Item Registry

All weapons, armour, books, materials, potions and accessories catalogued from across the world of Elucidate.

⚔ Weapons
Blunderbuss
Ranged
DMG 55Type Longranged
Function
The Mercenary's signature starting weapon. Two shots before reload — significant knockback on hit, essential for crowd control in multi-enemy scenarios and for breaking shield formations. Upgradeable to Reinforced Blunderbuss with Iron Ingot ×2 + Silver Wire.
Description
A flared brass muzzle that looks more like a trumpet than a firearm. Heavy, temperamental in moisture, but reliable when maintained. Blacksmith Conrad's words: She has never once let a man down who treated her right.
Lore
Issued by the Church's Third Regiment armory. The chest it came from is marked "Returned by nobody. As usual." Every soldier the Church sends through that armory takes a Blunderbuss. Most do not come back to return it. The ones who do come back changed.
Makeshift Spear
2-Handed
DMG 38Type Reach
Function
Long reach — keeps distance from Eldritch Horrors. Can be thrown once per combat encounter to lodge in an enemy and briefly immobilize them. Upgradeable to Iron Spear at any blacksmith. Becomes a liability in enclosed spaces like the Underground Laboratory.
Description
A knife strapped to a long stick with leather cord. The improvised nature is visible and irrelevant — the reach is real, and reach is what matters when what is coming toward you should not come close.
Lore
Craftable at Conrad's workshop after the introductory dialogue. The worn journal found in the back room of that workshop belongs to a soldier who also carried this spear and was sent to the Home Village. His last entry says something was wrong there for years. He was correct.
Scimitar
1-Handed
DMG 36Type Curved Blade
Function
Dropped by Cultist Soldiers. Single-edged, high damage for a one-handed weapon, best used where the Spear's reach is a disadvantage. Favored by the Cult for its cultural resonance — curved blades are associated in Cult theology with the arc of Caligo's influence through time.
Description
A wicked, sweeping curve of steel maintained with unusual care given the carriers. Cultist Soldiers keep their Scimitars ceremonially clean even when everything else they own is weathered. The edge is consistent proof of dedication.
Lore
The Cult adopted curved blades in the early years of their formation — a deliberate rejection of the Church's straight-sword iconography. What began as symbolism became tradition. The Scimitar the Mercenary might carry after Cultist Island was sharpened by someone who believed in something. That fact does not change what was done with it.
Flintlock
Ranged
DMG 46Type Longranged
Function
The Merchant's favored weapon. Faster to ready than the Blunderbuss, lower knockback. Best used for precision elimination rather than crowd suppression — Konrad's combat philosophy in one firearm.
Description
Compact, well-balanced, with wood grain smoothed by years of handling. The barrel has been modified — a small modification near the trigger guard that no standard gunsmith would recognize but that reduces misfires in damp conditions. Someone who uses this weapon has thought carefully about not dying.
Lore
Port City is a merchant city, and merchants in Port City carry Flintlocks not because they expect violence but because they have learned that expecting violence is irrelevant to whether it arrives. Konrad has carried his for fourteen years. He has fired it twice in that time. Both times were necessary. He does not enjoy the memory of either.
Short Sword
1-Handed
DMG 28Type Blade
Function
Purchasable from the Supply Merchant. Faster attack speed than the Spear. Best in enclosed spaces — corridors, the Underground Laboratory, the Cathedral's inner halls — where a longer weapon's reach becomes a hindrance against walls.
Description
A double-edged steel blade with a simple crossguard. Nothing ceremonial. Nothing wasted. The kind of weapon that was designed for the specific purpose of ending things quickly without announcement.
Lore
Standard issue for Church operatives working in close quarters. The Underground Laboratory encounters — where corridors were built to accommodate medical procedure, not combat — were the context for its design. The Church built the corridors narrow deliberately. They did not want two people to be able to pass side by side. Accountability requires witnesses.
Claymore
2-Handed
DMG 47Type Greatsword
Function
High-damage two-hander. The distinctive crossguard ending in quatrefoils is Church iconography — carrying it signals allegiance, or at minimum past allegiance, in ways that affect NPC dialogue in certain zones.
Description
Signifies both faith and a very long reach. The quatrefoils were added as a design choice three generations ago by a Church armorer who believed weapons should carry the marks of their purpose. The purpose was violence in service of doctrine. The ornamentation does not change that, but it makes it easier to perform.
Lore
Holy Knights carry Claymores. When the Mercenary fights former Church allies in Ending B, the weight of that specific weapon type in his inventory — if he is carrying one — triggers a unique internal monologue: recognition without sentimentality, a distinction the game insists upon. He has moved from using the weapon to carrying the memory of using it. That is different.
Cleaver
1-Handed
DMG 33Type Heavy Blade
Function
A wide, heavy swing weapon with significant stagger on hit. Particularly effective against lightly armored enemies and works well in close-quarters environments. The rectangular profile prevents it from being used in tight stabbing motions — a limitation that rewards positioning over finesse.
Description
A massive, rectangular slab of cast iron with a single purpose.
Lore
Originally a butcher's tool from the Port City market district. Its previous owner never had cause to use it as a weapon. The person who found it after the Port City riots did. The cleaver does not know the difference between a carcass and a cause — and neither did the war that followed.
Dagger
1-Handed
DMG 25Type Short Blade
Function
Fast attack speed with minimal telegraph. Ideal for situations requiring quick successive strikes. Concealable in inventory slots that larger weapons cannot occupy. Pairs with most one-handed off-hand options. The low damage per swing is offset by the speed at which additional swings can follow.
Description
A simple dagger.
Lore
The simplicity is the point. Everything in Elucidate carries history — the Dagger carries the honest history of things that are made to do exactly one thing and do it without ceremony. The Church distributed them to low-rank initiates who could not be trusted with a longsword yet. Most never needed to use them. A few did.
Longsword
2-Handed
DMG 32Type Blade
Function
Balanced attack profile with solid damage and adequate swing speed. The reliable choice for players seeking consistent one-handed performance without committing to heavier weapons. Compatible with shield use and effective across most enemy types encountered in the Theocratic Capital and surrounding zones.
Description
A straight, double-edged blade made out of cold-forged steel.
Lore
Cold-forging was banned by the Church's armory standard forty years ago — the process is slower and more expensive than smelting. The longswords still in circulation that were made this way are relics of a pre-Church manufacturing tradition. The blade remembers what the institution forgot about taking time to make something properly.
Iron Axe
1-Handed
DMG 30Type Axe
Function
Moderate damage with high penetration against layered armor. The axe head's geometry transfers force differently than a blade — useful against Holy Knights in plate configurations where edge weapons lose efficiency. Found commonly in village settlements and as a drop from Forest Patrol enemies.
Description
A tool used to cut wood but it does not mean that it can't cut a human.
Lore
The Home Village used iron axes for the lumber work that supplied Church construction. When the village was abandoned, the axes remained. Someone, later, found them. The description is written in the inventory as a statement of fact without emotion, which is its own kind of emotion.
Corsair's Saber
1-Handed
DMG 32Type Curved Blade
Function
Curved geometry offers superior slashing arcs compared to straight blades at the same damage tier. Found in Port City's harbor district and as a drop from coastal traders who have been pushed to desperation. The salt-resistance treatment on the blade means it does not degrade in moisture-heavy zones like the coastal passages.
Description
A curved, single-edged blade forged to resist the salt spray of the seas.
Lore
Port City's corsair trade collapsed when the Church imposed maritime restrictions. The blades remain — confiscated, redistributed, occasionally lost — making their way inland through channels that no longer exist. The saber carries the memory of open water even in landlocked rooms. Konrad recognizes the make. He does not say how.
Knife
1-Handed
DMG 22Type Short Blade
Function
Lowest damage among bladed one-handed weapons but retains all attack-speed advantages of the short-blade class. Useful as a secondary fallback when a primary weapon's durability is low. Throwable as a ranged attack for a single use before reverting to melee only — a mechanic shared with no other non-spear weapon.
Description
A single-edged steel blade.
Lore
Every cook, farmer, and tradesperson in the Elucidate world carries one. The knife does not ask to be a weapon. It is the most honest object in the game — designed for a purpose, repurposed by circumstance. The description is three words. Three words is all it needs.
Improvised Shiv
1-Handed
DMG 23Type Improvised
Function
Lowest-tier crafted weapon. Produced when no standard blade is available — requires only Leather Scraps and a Stick. Irregular edges cause a bleed-chance proc that standard knives do not. Its improvised nature means it cannot be upgraded at any blacksmith, but it bridges inventory gaps in early-game resource-scarce runs.
Description
A jagged piece of rebar wrapped in leather.
Lore
The Underground Laboratory has rebar. The rebar has jagged ends where the construction stopped mid-process and was never resumed. The leather wrapping was added by someone who intended to survive long enough to need a comfortable grip. Whoever they were, they left the shiv behind. The fact that it was left and not taken says something. No character in the game says what.
Steel Hammer
1-Handed
DMG 25Type Blunt
Function
Mid-tier blunt weapon. Toothed head increases trauma on armored foes — the serrations catch on plate seams and transfer additional force into the joint rather than glancing off the surface. Found in Conrad's workshop as a secondary item and dropped by Church construction crews encountered in the Theocratic Capital environs.
Description
A haft topped with a heavy, toothed metal head.
Lore
Conrad keeps three of them in his workshop. He does not explain why three. When asked, he says the first one is for work, the second is for emergencies, and the third is for if the emergency does not go well. He says it without smiling. He is not joking.
Stiletto
1-Handed
DMG 29Type Piercing
Function
High penetration against plated armor — the triangular or square cross-section wedges through gaps in plate that flat blades cannot enter. Dropped exclusively by Church Assassins, which ensures it appears only in mid-to-late game encounters. Not upgradeable through standard blacksmith routes — its specialized geometry requires a different materials path.
Description
A long, slender spike of triangular or square-sectioned steel.
Lore
Church Assassins carry two. The first is for the assignment. The second is for if something went wrong with the first. Their operational doctrine requires that every engagement end without testimony from the subject. The Stiletto's geometry is designed for exactly this — it leaves evidence of puncture but not of identity. The Church has spent considerable effort ensuring that the things it does cannot be named by what was left behind.
Dirk
1-Handed
DMG 26Type Long Blade
Function
Longer blade than the Dagger or Knife — reaches further while maintaining single-handed use. The heavy grip shifts balance toward the hand, reducing wrist fatigue in extended combat encounters. Favored by players who want the speed of a short weapon with slightly more reach than the standard short-blade class provides.
Description
A long, straight blade with a single sharp edge and a heavy grip.
Lore
The dirk was the personal sidearm of Church officers before the rank was abolished and the role folded into the Holy Knight classification. The officers are gone. The dirks remained in the armory, catalogued under a rank that no longer exists, issued to no one in particular and therefore available to anyone who knew where to look.
Maul
2-Handed
DMG 43Type Two-Handed Blunt
Function
Matched damage to the Spear but with a dramatically different attack profile — short range, massive stagger, effective against shielded enemies where the blunt mass bypasses shield edge and breaks stance. The slowest swing arc in the two-handed category. Each swing is a commitment. Missing with the Maul has consequences.
Description
A massive, six-sided block of reinforced iron welded to an ash-wood pole.
Lore
Six sides is deliberate — each face of the head is a different weight distribution, meaning no two strikes land quite the same way. This was an engineering choice made by the Blacksmith's Guild before the Guild was absorbed into the Church's production mandate. The Maul is one of the last objects they made on their own terms. The ash-wood pole is original. It has not been replaced.
Spear
2-Handed
DMG 43Type Reach
Function
Upgraded successor to the Makeshift Spear. Retains the long-reach advantage and one throwable use per combat encounter — the thrown Spear now lodges and deals tick damage rather than simple immobilization. Properly forged, unlike its Makeshift counterpart, meaning it can be upgraded at Conrad's workshop for further stat increases.
Description
Seven feet of seasoned ash tipped with a leaf-shaped steel head.
Lore
Distinct from the Makeshift Spear in every way that matters — where the Makeshift is improvised survival, the Spear is craft. Seasoned ash means the wood was cut and dried for two years before being shaped. Leaf-shaped heads are an older design, pre-Church, from a military tradition the Church does not acknowledge in its doctrine. The weapon remembers a history it was not permitted to have.
Short Bow
Ranged
DMG 27Type Longranged
Function
Entry-level ranged weapon. Fastest draw speed among bows — essential for the Marksmanship and Rapid Fire skill chains. Small frame allows use in enclosed areas where the Longbow would catch walls and ceilings. Effective in Zones of Terrors where maintaining distance is more valuable than maximizing damage output per shot.
Description
A recurved frame small enough to be slung over a shoulder without catching on low-hanging branches.
Lore
The Tribe used Short Bows for two purposes — hunting and ceremony — and considered them the same act approached from different directions. Pluvia's early combat options reflect this: the bow was not her first weapon. It was her first tool. The distinction, in Tribe practice, matters significantly.
Longbow
Ranged
DMG 29Type Longranged
Function
Greater effective range than the Short Bow with improved damage per arrow. Draw time is longer — the Rapid Fire skill book mitigates this significantly. Best used in open environments like the Plains zones and Coastal Cliffs where the additional range pays off. Awkward in the Underground Laboratory's tight corridors.
Description
A simple weapon, yet it transformed the battlefields of the world.
Lore
The description is unusually historical for an item entry. No character in the game identifies who wrote the inventory text — it reads like an excerpt from a document that predates the Theocratic Capital's founding. The Church suppressed historical military texts to prevent soldiers from learning that the battles they were told never happened did, in fact, happen. The Longbow knows better.
Heavy Crossbow
Ranged
DMG 26Type Longranged
Function
Lower raw damage than the bows but the bolt travels faster and with a flatter trajectory — more reliable over distance against moving targets. Reload time is the longest of any ranged weapon. Ideal for players who prefer to guarantee a hit over taking multiple fast shots. The mechanical draw means Strength modifiers from heavy armor builds apply to bolt velocity.
Description
A compact yet deadly ranged weapon that trades speed for devastating precision.
Lore
The Church's siege division used Heavy Crossbows for garrison defense — positions where reload time was less critical than shot certainty. When the siege division was disbanded, the crossbows were supposed to be destroyed. The armory records show thirty-seven destroyed. The armory originally held forty-two. The five unaccounted for appear in the game world in places the Church has no record of putting them.
Musket
Ranged
DMG 50Type Longranged
Function
Highest ranged damage among standard weapons — surpassed only by the Blunderbuss at close range. Single shot before a lengthy reload. The long barrel provides maximum accuracy at the cost of usability in tight spaces. Best deployed as an opener for high-value single targets before switching to a secondary weapon. The Marksmanship passive skill book directly enhances Musket performance.
Description
A long-barreled firearm built for battlefield volleys and disciplined marksmen.
Lore
The Church's Third Regiment trained with Muskets in formation — volleys from disciplined ranks rather than individual marksmanship. The regiment was deployed once, at the Coastal Settlement incident, and the records of what they fired at have been removed from the Archives. The Muskets were returned cleaned. The people who cleaned them did not file reports.
🛡 Armour
Priest's Robe
Body
DEF 8Mind Res +1
Function
Dropped by Father Calum upon defeat in Ending B. Low physical defense, but the Mind Resistance bonus reflects what wearing the Church's authority does to a person's psychological armor — even stripped from the man who abused it. Equipping it triggers a unique reaction from any surviving NPCs who recognize it.
Description
White, or once white — now off-white in the way that long-held certainty becomes off-white when it is finally examined. Embroidered with the Church seal at the hem. The stitching is immaculate. Someone who cared about this robe cared about it deeply before the caring became the mechanism of something terrible.
Lore
Father Calum was dressed as C-1 before he was dressed as a priest. The robe was given to him when the Church decided that the most successful product of their program should be the program's administrator. He has worn it every day for thirty years. His personal journal's last entry ends mid-sentence. The robe outlasts the sentence.
Dark Priest's Robe
Body
DEF 8Speed +1
Function
Dropped by Cultist higher-ups. Trades Mind Resistance for Speed — the Cult's philosophy in a stat line. They move faster because they stopped trying to hold their psychological ground and started moving with the current. Whether that is wisdom or collapse is the game's central question.
Description
Black, well-maintained despite the conditions of Cultist Island. The embroidery is Caligo's sigil — not the stylized version used in Cult propaganda, but the original form, which the Cult's founding members transcribed from their earliest Fragment interactions. It looks like a question mark that has lost its curve.
Lore
The Cult adopted black robes as a direct negation of the Church's white — but over time, the symbolism inverted. White for the Church means purity of purpose; black for the Cult came to mean transparency about what they are: instruments of something that does not care about them, who chose that relationship over the alternative of pretending the caring was real.
Body Plate Armour
Body
DEF 28
Function
High physical protection, no secondary effects. Best in structured combat zones — the Theocratic Capital Ending B, the port landings — where physical damage is predictable and consistent. Slows movement in ways that make it inadvisable in the Zones of Terrors where evasion matters more than absorption.
Description
Made from different alloys, offering moderate to high protection. The craftsmanship is undeniably skilled. The Church funds good armor because dead soldiers are expensive; the investment is purely logistical. The soldiers inside the armor draw whatever comfort they can from that.
Lore
Church-issue Plate Armour bears no personal markings — soldiers are not supposed to be individuals inside it. This was a deliberate design decision documented in a Church administrative bulletin titled Standardization of Field Equipment for Identity Consistency. The bulletin is seventeen pages long. The reason it takes seventeen pages to explain why armor should not be personalized is its own kind of lore.
Coif
Head
DEF 7Type Head Armour
Function
Minimal defense, maximum comfort. The Coif's soft linen construction provides negligible physical protection but is the only head slot item that does not affect Mind State readings in the game's passive anxiety system — wearing heavier helmets in psychologically charged zones incrementally increases MS through a "confinement" modifier. The Coif bypasses this entirely.
Description
A white linen bonnet-like cap.
Lore
Church initiates wore Coifs during their first year of service — considered a mark of incomplete formation, removed once the initiate received their full kit. The Coif was not designed to protect. It was designed to identify someone who was still becoming what the Church wanted them to be. Several characters in Elucidate are still, in some sense, wearing theirs.
Iron Helmet
Head
DEF 20Type Head Armour
Function
Solid mid-to-high tier head protection. Cast iron construction provides reliable defense without the weight penalties of forged plate variants. The standard Church line infantry helmet — well distributed across the mid-game as a drop item, meaning it becomes accessible at a point where its DEF 20 rating represents a meaningful upgrade over leather alternatives.
Description
A helmet made out of cast iron.
Lore
Cast from Church foundry iron — the same ore mined in the northern territories. The helmet was made to a specification written forty years ago and has not been revised since. It protects exactly as well as it was designed to protect. The soldiers wearing it have changed. The helmet has not. The Church finds consistency in materials easier to maintain than consistency in people.
Cloth Hood
Head
DEF 5Type Head Armour
Function
Marginally better than the Coif in physical defense, similar in spirit. Provides the same confinement-modifier exemption in MS-sensitive zones as the Coif — the cloth class of head armour shares this property. Ideal for Mind State management runs where preserving psychological stability is more valuable than incremental defense gains.
Description
A helmet made of cloth.
Lore
Calling it a helmet is the inventory system being charitable. It is a hood. It covers the head. It does not protect what it covers. The people in Elucidate who wear cloth hoods do so because they had nothing else, or because they chose comfort over armor, or because something happened to their real helmet and they kept moving anyway. All three histories are valid. All three are present in the game world.
Chainmail Hood
Head
DEF 13Type Head Armour
Function
Flexible linked-metal construction specifically effective against arrow and bolt impacts — the interlocking rings distribute puncture force across a broader area. Its anti-ranged specialization makes it uniquely valuable in Cultist Island encounters where archers are common. Lower effective defense against blunt weapons compared to its raw DEF number suggests.
Description
A hood made to protect people from arrows.
Lore
The Cult's archers were trained at a different doctrine than the Church's — aimed at specific targets rather than area coverage. The Chainmail Hood was developed in response to early conflicts where Cult arrows came from unexpected angles. Knowing what someone developed their armor to stop tells you a great deal about what they expected to encounter. The Church expected arrows from people who knew where to aim.
Plate Helmet
Head
DEF 22Type Head Armour
Function
High-defense head armour available in the mid-to-late game. Heavy construction provides the highest physical defense in the head slot outside of the Guard Bascinet. Pairs with Plate Armour and Iron Cuirass for a cohesive heavy-armour build. The enclosed visor reduces peripheral vision — a minor mechanical penalty on dodge-timing checks.
Description
A heavy armour made to protect its wearer.
Lore
The Plate Helmet is the point at which armor stops being practical and starts being a statement. Something fully encased in plate is telling the world that it expects to be struck and has made peace with that expectation. The Church's elite soldiers wear it not because they fear being hit — they wear it so that whatever strikes them does not finish the sentence it started.
Iron Mask
Head
DEF 12Type Head Armour
Function
Face-coverage only — the Iron Mask leaves the crown and back of the head unprotected. Its DEF 12 applies exclusively to frontal face impacts, making it most effective as a supplementary piece for characters already wearing a hood or coif. Unique among head armour: removes the character's facial expressions from NPC reaction calculations in social encounters.
Description
A mask made out of cast iron.
Lore
The Iron Mask was used in the Church's detention program — not worn by guards, but fitted on subjects during transport to remove their identity from public record. A masked subject in transit was administratively a package, not a person. The masks were supposed to be destroyed after use. Several were not. They appear in the game world in places that were once transit points. The Church has thorough records of where they should be. Less thorough records of where they are.
Guard Bascinet
Head
DEF 25Type Head Armour
Function
Highest defense in the head slot. The integrated throat and neck guard extends protection into the upper body where standard helmets leave a gap. Dropped exclusively by Elite Guard enemies in the Theocratic Capital's inner sanctum — encountered late-game only. The throat is the most exposed point in a standard helmet configuration — the Guard Bascinet's designers understood that. 📖 Books & Skill Texts
Book of Craftsmanship
Passive Skill
Function
Reading unlocks the Craftsmanship ability, enabling item crafting at any blacksmith station. Available in the Walled City armory chest and again in the Dormitory Ruins of the Home Village. Foundational — almost every weapon upgrade requires it.
Lore
The Walled City copy was left in the armory by the Third Regiment, unclaimed. The copy in the Home Village ruins has marginal notes in a child's handwriting. The notes are practical observations about metalwork. Whoever wrote them was paying close attention. The Church had not yet taught them not to.
Book of Stars
Active Skill
Function
Unlocks the Foresight skill. Reading it costs MS +5 — the knowledge of what is coming is not free. Foresight reveals enemy positions, trap locations, and hidden NPC information. Its reliability degrades with MS: perfect clarity below 50, functional above 50, dangerously unreliable above 75.
Lore
Pluvia's people did not learn Foresight from this book. They held the knowledge before the book existed. The book is a Church-adjacent transcription of something the Tribe understood natively — translated into academic language that removes the felt sense of it entirely and replaces it with procedure. Pluvia, if asked about the book, says: "Reading that is like being given a map of a country you have already walked."
Book of Instincts
Active Skill
Function
Unlocks Battle Instinct — briefly marks enemy weak points for amplified damage. Father Calum uses this skill in Phase 2 of his boss fight. For player characters, it is particularly valuable against heavily armored targets where exploiting openings matters more than raw damage output.
Lore
The Church produced this book internally. It was written by the program administrators to codify what the experiments produced — instincts that had been trained into subjects so deeply they could no longer be separated from identity. Father Calum co-authored it. He is listed as a technical consultant. His co-author was Subject C-2, who did not survive the procedure that made Battle Instinct possible to document.
Book of Trade I — III
Passive Skill
Function
A series of three books unlocking Bartering, Bargaining, and Rumours respectively. Konrad begins with Bartering; the full chain reduces purchase prices and opens alternative information-gathering in tavern interactions. Book of Trade III (Rumours) unlocks NPC whisper dialogue unavailable through standard conversation.
Lore
The Guild Hall stocks all three. The Guild Master who warns Konrad about pressing the Church investigation has read all three volumes many times. He knows what they are for. He also knows that information has a price paid not in coins but in the kind of quiet that follows knowing too much. He made his choice twenty years ago and lives with it. Konrad made his more recently.
Book of the Secrets
Lore Book
Function
Reading grants no skill — only knowledge. The game's most explicit lore item regarding Church hypocrisy: it states plainly that the Church worships a false God while actually embracing darkness. No mechanics attached. Some information changes nothing and everything simultaneously.
Lore
The book exists in the Restricted Corridor of the Theocratic Capital, behind a door that Father Calum opens with a key he carries on his person at all times. He has not read this book in eleven years, by choice. It was written by one of the original Church founders — someone who built the institution knowing exactly what they were building and chose the name Lumen as irony, not faith. The founder's name has been removed from every historical record. Someone named F has written in the margin: "Both cages. Still cages."
Book of Healing
Active Skill
Function
Unlocks the Healing active skill, allowing recovery of HP outside combat. The description calls it a book that teaches the doctrine of Lumen — meaning the Church's canonical theology is the instruction manual for keeping yourself alive. The game does not comment on this irony directly. It simply states it.
Lore
The Lumen doctrine teaches that light cures corruption. The experiments the Church ran on children suggest a different interpretation: that what they called light was the process of removing whatever the darkness had touched, regardless of whether removing it left anything behind. The Book of Healing teaches restoration. What the Church practiced was subtraction. These are different things that the book does not reconcile, because the book was written before the experiments began.
Book of Marksmanship
Skill Book
Type Passive Skill BookEffect Teaches Marksmanship
Function
Reading this book permanently unlocks the Marksmanship passive skill. Marksmanship increases effective accuracy and critical-hit chance for all ranged weapons — bows, crossbows, and firearms. The passive activates without equipping and stacks with the Rapid Fire active skill. Essential for ranged-focused builds and required for optimal Musket performance.
Description
A book about the art of marksmanship.
Lore
The Church maintained a dedicated marksmanship training program for two decades before the regiment was reorganized. The curriculum was formalized in this text — a practical guide that treats aiming as philosophy: patience, breath, the relationship between stillness and movement. Whoever wrote it understood that hitting a target reliably requires understanding why you are missing first. The Church used the text. They did not learn the lesson in the last chapter.
Book of Rapid Fire
Skill Book
Type Active Skill BookEffect Teaches Rapid Fire
Function
Unlocks the Rapid Fire active skill upon reading. Rapid Fire dramatically reduces draw and reload time for all bow-class weapons for a limited duration — transforming the Short Bow from a controlled precision tool into a sustained suppression weapon. Consumes stamina while active. Does not apply to crossbow or firearm weapons, which have mechanical rather than muscular draw cycles.
Description
A book detailing how to draw a bow faster.
Lore
Speed in archery is counterintuitive — drawing faster requires more controlled relaxation of the draw arm, not more force. This book exists because that counterintuition needs to be written down; it does not come naturally to soldiers trained to associate effort with result. Someone figured out the contradiction and documented it. The chapter on breathing is longer than the chapter on technique. This is correct.
Book of Agility
Skill Book
Type Active Skill BookEffect Teaches Agility Augmentation
Function
Unlocks Agility Augmentation — an active skill that temporarily boosts dodge speed, attack animation recovery, and general movement speed simultaneously. The combined effect creates a brief window of near-untouchable mobility ideal for repositioning in multi-enemy encounters. Pairs with Swift Boots for a combined speed effect that significantly changes how combat flows.
Description
A book about the art of being fast in battles.
Lore
The author's position is stated in the preface: speed is not a natural gift distributed unequally, but a skill set developed through structured understanding of how the body moves when it is afraid versus when it is not. The book is about reducing the fear-response lag — the fraction of a second between perceiving danger and acting on it. In Elucidate, that fraction of a second is represented mechanically. The book closes it.
Book of Cowardice I
Skill Book
Type Active Skill BookEffect Teaches Escape
Function
Unlocks the Escape active skill — a one-use-per-encounter ability that immediately removes the character from melee range with a guaranteed distance break regardless of enemy AI state. Essential for encounters where the combat math does not favor engagement. Particularly useful in the Underground Laboratory's ambush configurations where initial positioning is already compromised.
Description
A book about escaping if the battle is against your odds.
Lore
The title is intentional. Whoever named this text called it cowardice knowing that the word would sting, and wrote it anyway, because what they were teaching was more important than whether readers were comfortable with the framing. Knowing when you cannot win and acting on that knowledge requires a different kind of courage than staying. The Church does not teach this. The Church teaches soldiers to stay. Some of those soldiers did not need to.
Book of Cowardice II
Skill Book
Type Active Skill BookEffect Teaches Sprint
Function
Unlocks Sprint — an active skill providing a sustained movement speed burst with extended duration compared to the base dodge-roll. Where Escape is for instant disengagement, Sprint is for creating sustained distance. Ideal for the open zones where pursuit AI has a longer tracking range. Stacks with Swift Boots for combined mobility that most enemy AI cannot match at full speed.
Description
A book about how running away from a battle is ok.
Lore
The second volume makes the philosophical argument explicit that the first only implied: survival is legitimate. Running away is not failure; it is the preservation of the capacity to make different choices later. The author does not argue against fighting. They argue against fighting when fighting is the only option that has been made available. The distinction is the entire book. The description is its thesis, stated plainly. Running away from a battle is ok. Someone needed to write that down.
Book of Pestilence I
Pestilence
Type Status BookEffect Bleeding
Function
Reading this book inflicts the Bleeding status on the reader. Bleeding causes continuous HP loss per turn until treated with appropriate materials. The status cannot be resisted through equipment — it bypasses all DEF values and interacts directly with the HP pool. The only counter is Green Herb-based remedies. Do not read unless the consequence is understood.
Description
Causes bleeding when read.
Lore
The eight Books of Pestilence are the most unambiguous items in the game. The description is a warning. The warning is accurate. There is no hidden benefit, no conditional upside, no lore reward for reading them. They are a series of traps written in book form — the form chosen deliberately, because books imply knowledge and knowledge implies improvement. These do not improve the reader. The author understood this. The author did it anyway. That is all the lore the Book of Pestilence offers. It is enough.
⚠ Inflicts Bleeding immediately on reading. Effect cannot be blocked by armour. Treat with Green Herb compounds or Betadine.
Book of Pestilence II
Pestilence
Type Status BookEffect Infection
Function
Reading inflicts Infection status. Infection is a slower-acting status than Bleeding — it does not cause immediate HP loss but instead reduces the effectiveness of all healing items by 50% and incrementally degrades DEF values over time. Treated with Betadine or Antibiotics. If left untreated through multiple encounters, Infection escalates to a more severe compound status.
Description
Causes infection when read.
Lore
Of the eight volumes, Infection is the one most consistent with the Church's documented methods. The Underground Laboratory's records include infection as an experimental vector — studied, catalogued, and weaponized in controlled conditions. The Book of Pestilence II did not emerge from nowhere. It emerged from notes. Someone read those notes and decided to put what they learned into a form anyone could pick up.
⚠ Inflicts Infection on reading. Reduces healing efficiency. Treat with Betadine or Antibiotics before escalation.
Book of Pestilence III
Pestilence
Type Status BookEffect Broken Bones
Function
Inflicts Broken Bones on reading. This status removes the Sprint ability if learned, halves movement speed, and imposes a damage penalty on physical melee attacks due to compromised striking form. Unlike Bleeding or Infection, Broken Bones has no single-item cure — it requires resting at a safe zone and is one of the only statuses not addressable through inventory management alone.
Description
Causes broken bones when read.
Lore
The most physically visceral of the pestilence statuses. A book that breaks bones through the act of reading it operates in the same territory as the Fragments of Caligo — reality responding to attention in ways that have no precedent in natural physics. The Pestilence series was not written by the Church. The Underground Laboratory records reference "text-form status delivery" as an experimental approach. The experiment produced results.
⚠ Broken Bones cannot be cured with inventory items. Requires rest at a safe zone. Avoid equipping before major encounters.
Book of Pestilence IV
Pestilence
Type Status BookEffect Stun
Function
Inflicts Stun immediately on reading. Stun locks all player inputs for a fixed duration — during this window the character cannot dodge, attack, use items, or interact with the environment. The duration is long enough to be dangerous in any active combat zone. Reading this book in a safe area and waiting out the stun is the only responsible approach.
Description
Causes stun when read.
Lore
The Stun the book delivers is physical — not psychological, not metaphorical. The words on the page produce a neurological response in the reader that interrupts motor function. This is the point at which the Pestilence series shifts from harmful-but-explicable to something the game's internal logic struggles to contain. Knowledge that directly causes paralysis is not knowledge functioning normally. It is something that uses reading as a delivery mechanism for something else entirely.
⚠ Stun takes effect instantly. Do not read in active combat zones. Wait out the duration in a cleared area.
Book of Pestilence V
Pestilence
Type Status BookEffect Confusion
Function
Inflicts Confusion on reading. Confusion randomizes directional inputs — movement commands are remapped unpredictably for the duration of the status. Additionally, item use becomes unreliable: there is a chance that using an item while Confused activates the wrong item from the inventory. MS accumulation rate increases during Confusion as a secondary effect.
Description
Causes confusion when read.
Lore
Confusion is Caligo's most recognizable signature — the entity operates by disrupting the coherence of meaning, and the Book of Pestilence V delivers a miniaturized version of that disruption through text. The Underground Laboratory experimented with Caligo-derived disorientation as a control mechanism. The book is the result of someone taking that experiment and making it portable. The experiment was classified. The book is not.
⚠ Confusion remaps directional inputs and disrupts item use. MS increases during the status duration. Do not read in proximity to enemies.
Book of Pestilence VI
Pestilence
Type Status BookEffect Blindness
Function
Inflicts Blindness on reading. The game's visual output is partially obscured for the duration — the peripheral view darkens and direct-line visibility shortens significantly. Enemy indicators do not appear through the blindness effect. The character can still function but relies entirely on audio cues and memory of the environment. This is the most disorienting status in the series from a player experience standpoint.
Description
Causes blindness when read.
Lore
There is something specific about blindness caused by reading — the organ that processed the text becomes the organ that is damaged by it. The Pestilence series has a pattern of this: the mechanism of the harm and the mechanism of delivery are the same. You use your hands to open the book. You use your eyes to read it. The book uses your eyes to take something from you. This is deliberate. Someone designed this. That fact is not discussed by any character in the game.
⚠ Blindness significantly reduces visibility. Enemy indicators are disabled. Audio cues become the primary navigation tool during the status duration.
Book of Pestilence VII
Pestilence
Type Status BookEffect Critical
Function
Inflicts Critical status on reading. Critical places the character in a state where any additional damage — from any source, any amount — triggers a critical health threshold response: HP is immediately reduced to 1. The Critical status is the most dangerous in the series from a death-risk perspective; a single incidental hit becomes a near-death event. Blue Gems can suppress the effect but not remove it.
Description
Causes critical when read.
Lore
The Critical status is not a physical wound — it is a change in the body's relationship to being struck. Something in the text recalibrates what a hit means. This is the closest the Pestilence series comes to operating at a metaphysical level rather than a physical one. Whatever Caligo does to the minds of those it reaches through the Fragment encounters, the Book of Pestilence VII does a version of the same thing to the body's damage threshold. The entity's influence has multiple vocabularies.
⚠ Any damage taken while Critical triggers immediate near-death. Blue Gems suppress but do not remove the status. Avoid all combat until the status clears.
Book of Pestilence VIII
Pestilence
Type Status BookEffect Infected
Function
Reading inflicts Infected — the compound, escalated form of Infection, distinct in that it cannot be treated with Betadine or Antibiotics alone. Infected requires the combination of Green Herb, Blue Herb, and Empty Vial to produce the compound remedy. It compounds the effects of Infection with an MS accumulation increase and removes the effectiveness of all HP regeneration during its duration.
Description
Causes infected when read.
Lore
The eighth volume is the culmination. Each of the seven preceding books delivered a discrete harm. The Infected status compounds and escalates — it is the series' acknowledgment that the individual harms were practice. Whatever the Pestilence books are, they build toward something. The eighth is not a conclusion. It is an arrival point for damage that was accumulating since the first volume. Finding all eight in sequence and reading them — something the game does not prevent — produces a character state the designers specifically named. They named it Infected. They meant something by it beyond the physical.
⚠ Infected requires a compound remedy — Green Herb + Blue Herb + Empty Vial. Standard medicines are insufficient. MS increases during duration. HP regeneration is disabled.
◇ Materials & Crafting Components
Blue Gem
Material
Function
The game's primary MS reducer. Critical to stockpile before entering doubled-rate zones like the Home Village. The Shaman's playthrough begins with one in the Healing Hut — its placement is the game's quiet acknowledgment that Pluvia's starting MS of 30 is already precarious. Not having Blue Gems when the MS escalation hits is the most common cause of Path C triggers.
Description
A blue-colored gem of unknown mineral composition. Its calming effect is not metaphorical — it produces a measurable decrease in MS that no in-game character can explain and no Church document accounts for. Caligo is affected by it the same way weather is affected by a clear window: it does not stop, but something about the barrier makes it matter less.
Lore
Blue Gems appear naturally in Tribe territory and near coastal regions outside Church jurisdiction. They do not appear near the Theocratic Capital, the Underground Laboratory, or Cultist Island. Whether this is geological coincidence or something more purposeful is not stated in any document. Pluvia, if asked directly, says: "Some things resist without choosing to. The gem is one of them." She says it the way someone says something they believe is true but cannot prove.
Iron Ingot
Material
Function
Primary crafting material for weapon upgrades. Two Iron Ingots plus Silver Wire upgrades the Blunderbuss to its Reinforced variant. Found in the Blacksmith Workshop's locked cabinet and dropped by Holy Knights and Church Soldiers. The most common crafting component across all upgrade trees.
Lore
The Church's foundries run continuously. Iron comes from mines in the northern territories — the same territories where the Home Village sits. The village that was destroyed provided iron ore to the Church before it became a site of Eldritch Horror incursion. The supply chain continued for two years after the village was abandoned. The Church marked it as a "supply disruption" in their records, not a tragedy.
Silver Wire
Material
Function
Used in accessory crafting and the Blunderbuss upgrade. Dropped by Church Assassins — the most elite and dangerous of the Church's mobile operatives. The fact that assassins carry refined silver wire is not coincidence; it is part of their toolkit for weapon modification in the field.
Lore
Silver has traditional significance in Lumen doctrine — associated with clarity and precision, qualities the Church values in instruments that do its work quietly. Church Assassins are not listed in any official Church roster. They are funded through the same administrative category that covers "Charitable Operations." The Silver Wire they carry is listed as a "blessed implement." The Church has creative language for most things.
Green Herb
Material
Function
Crafting material said to cure infections. Combined with Empty Vials for medicinal compounds. Found commonly in Tribe territory and scattered through the Zones of Terrors — nature persisting in corrupted places, which the game uses as consistent background symbolism.
Lore
The Tribe's Shaman has used Green Herbs in her practice for decades. The Church's medical staff filed a report fourteen years ago recommending the standardization of Tribe herbal knowledge into Church medical protocol. The report was rejected. The rejection note reads: "Sourcing through non-institutional channels creates accountability gaps." People in the Home Village could have used the herbs. They were not given access to the protocol.
Red Gem
Material
Function
Said to contain the soul of a great warrior. Rare drop in high-risk zones. Used in accessory crafting — specifically in combinations that amplify offensive capability at the cost of psychological stability. The trade-off is mechanical and also thematic.
Lore
The Red Gem does not contain a soul. It contains a resonance — a pattern from something that was alive that impressed itself into the crystal's structure at the moment of that thing's death. Whether that qualifies as a soul is the kind of question the Cult's Library of Caligo addresses in seventeen competing marginal notes from seven different readers, none of whom agree, all of whom have circled the same passage in the original text.
Leather Scraps
Material
Function
Common crafting material for armor repairs and upgrades. Dropped by Church Soldiers and found throughout the Walled City. Required for the Iron Spear upgrade alongside Iron Ingots.
Lore
Church Soldiers patch their own equipment in the field using Leather Scraps from a standard-issue repair kit. This was not always policy — previously, soldiers returned to armories for repairs. The change was made after too many soldiers used armory visits to speak to blacksmiths like Conrad. Conrad, notably, still has his old appointment logs. He has kept them for twenty years. He is the kind of man who keeps things.
Paper
Material
Type Material
Function
Basic crafting material — used in the production of written items and in recipes requiring a blank substrate. Distinct from Ancient Paper, which is a singular item with fixed lore significance. Standard Paper is a common drop and can be found throughout the world in civilian locations. Multiple units stack in inventory.
Description
A blank piece of paper.
Lore
Paper in Elucidate is not neutral. Everything that has been done to people in this world was first written on paper — the Church's directives, the Laboratory's experiment logs, the suppressed reports, the procurement orders for the Home Village's resources that continued after the village was abandoned. Blank paper is just the space before that. It is the only truly innocent material in the crafting system. What is made from it may not be.
Raw Iron
Material
Type Material
Function
Precursor to Iron Ingot — Raw Iron must be processed at Conrad's forge before it can be used in weapon and armor crafting recipes. Found in mine locations and northern territory zones. Not directly usable in any recipe in its raw state. The processing step at the forge is intentional — it represents a crafting pipeline rather than instant availability of refined materials.
Description
A raw iron found in the wild.
Lore
The northern territories where Raw Iron is found are the same territories as the Home Village. The mines predated the village, and the village grew around the mining work. When the village was abandoned, the mines were not — the Church contracted outside labor to continue extraction. The Raw Iron in the player's inventory may have come from a mine worked by someone who replaced a person no longer able to work it. That transaction is not documented anywhere.
Wooden Plank
Material
Type Material
Function
Primary crafting component for the Wooden Buckler and several environmental interaction recipes. Found abundantly in building sites, abandoned structures, and woodcutting locations. The most accessible structural material in the game — early-game crafting relies on Wooden Plank combinations when iron is not yet available.
Description
A piece of wooden plank.
Lore
The Home Village's primary export was lumber processed into planks for Church construction in the capital. The Iron Axes found in the village were used for this work. Wooden Planks from that village went into the walls of buildings in the Theocratic Capital — buildings the player walks through later in the game. The game does not annotate this. It does not need to. The plank is in the wall. The village is gone. The building is standing.
Bowstring
Material
Type Material
Function
Required component for crafting and upgrading bow-class weapons. A Short Bow requires one Bowstring plus a Stick and additional components. The Bowstring's fiber composition affects the final weapon's draw-weight rating — higher-quality fibers produce bows with faster arrow velocity. Found in hunting supply caches and Tribe territory storage locations.
Description
A fiber made to be used as a spring for arrows.
Lore
The Tribe's bowstrings were made from plant fiber processed through a specific treatment the Church's armory never successfully replicated. Church-produced bowstrings break at higher frequency under the same load. The Tribe knew this. The Church's armory reports documented this. The reports recommended acquiring Tribe manufacturing knowledge through formal exchange. The recommendation was not acted on. The Church continued to use inferior bowstrings. Some things are ideologically expensive to fix.
Blue Herb
Material
Type Material
Function
Rarer than the Green Herb and required specifically for the compound remedy against the Infected status — one of two herbs required alongside an Empty Vial. Also used in crafting MS-reduction compounds when processed with Blue Gem material. Found in Tribe territory and high-altitude zones outside Church jurisdiction. The Shaman's extended dialogue references it by a different name in Tribe language.
Description
A plant that is said to be able to cure all your fatigues.
Lore
Fatigue in Elucidate is not a singular condition — it is the accumulation of everything the game world does to the characters who move through it. The Blue Herb does not cure that. The description says it is said to — the hedge is deliberate. No plant cures everything. The Tribe knows this better than the description suggests. Pluvia, asked about the Blue Herb in extended dialogue, says what it actually does is make the body remember it has rested, even when it has not. That is not nothing.
Stick
Material
Type Material
Function
Base crafting component for the Makeshift Spear and Short Bow crafting trees. The most abundant material in the game — found on the ground in any forested or outdoor zone. Its ubiquity means early-game crafting is never gated by availability of this component. Several crafting chains begin here and scale upward: Stick becomes weapon becomes upgrade.
Description
A Stick.
Lore
The inventory description is capitalized and nothing else. A Stick. The inventory text system applies the same descriptive effort to a Stick as it does to the Fragments of Caligo — both get an entry, both get a description. The Stick's description is three characters plus a period. This is either the writer reaching the end of their patience or a deliberate comment on the economy of attention: not everything needs its story told. Some things are just sticks. The world contains them. You pick them up.
Empty Vial
Material
Type Material
Function
Container component required for any liquid-based crafting recipe. Essential for the Infected compound remedy — without an Empty Vial, the Green Herb and Blue Herb cannot be combined into a usable form. Also used in producing custom MS-reduction compounds in later crafting tiers. Found in the Underground Laboratory, apothecary locations, and Tribe storage.
Description
An empty glass vial.
Lore
The Underground Laboratory produced a significant quantity of empty vials — the experiment logs reference hundreds of compounds tested in glass vessels. Most of those vessels were destroyed with their contents after each experiment. Some were not. The Empty Vials in the game world that come from the Laboratory carry the residue of whatever was last in them. The residue is not visible. The crafting system does not differentiate between a clean vial and one that has held something that should not have existed. Perhaps it should.
⚗ Potions & Consumables
Red Vial
Potion
Effect HP +70
Function
Powerful instant HP recovery. The most efficient healing item in the game — 70 HP restored immediately with no side effects. Scarce in early zones; more available after the Port City. Essential stockpile item for the Cultist Island assault.
Lore
Church medical reports refer to Red Vials as accelerated coagulant compound with neural stabilizing properties. The same compound appears in the Underground Laboratory's supply manifests listed under a different name: Subject Stabilization Fluid. The applications differ. The formula is identical. The Church is meticulous about language in exactly the places where language is doing the most work.
Ale / Rum / Wine
Drink
Effect MS -10
Function
All three alcohol variants reduce MS by 10. A tactical option in zones where Blue Gems have been depleted. The Tavern Keeper at The Gilded Anchor keeps them stocked and notices who drinks what quantity — his tracking of Konrad's dark ale consumption is how he knows Konrad is carrying something heavy.
Lore
The game acknowledges without judgment that alcohol as a coping mechanism for psychological pressure is both understandable and insufficient. Ale reduces MS. It does not resolve what caused the MS to rise. The Tavern Keeper, who has observed this dynamic for twenty years, puts Konrad's second drink on the house. He does not say this is because he cares. He does not need to say it.
Betadine / Antibiotics
Medicine
Effect Remove Infection
Function
Both items cure the Infection status — Betadine for wound cleaning, Antibiotics for systemic infection. The Zones of Terrors have environmental hazards that inflict Infection through contact with corrupted surfaces. Having either item available is essential in high-risk exploration.
Lore
The Tribe's healing traditions use neither. They use Green Herbs, specific clay, and Pluvia's guidance. The Church brought Betadine and Antibiotics into common use — genuine medical contributions that made people's lives better. This is acknowledged in the game's lore and not used to excuse anything. Good work and terrible work can come from the same institution. That is part of what makes the institution difficult to simply condemn and leave.
💎 Accessories
Blue Amulet
Accessory
Mind Res +1
Function
Passive Mind Resistance increase. Worn, it slows MS accumulation throughout the playthrough — modest but consistent. Particularly useful for Funeris and Pluvia, whose base MS resistance is 1/5, the lowest in the game.
Lore
Strange sense of calm. That is the item description's full text. In a game that documents everything in clinical or literary detail, the Blue Amulet has seven words of description and no lore entry in any document. The Tribe Elder, if asked about it, says only: "Some things do not explain themselves. They simply are." This is either profound or the game acknowledging it ran out of origin text for one item. The calm is real either way.
Ring
Accessory
DEF 1
Function
Minimal stats — 1 defense. But equipping it triggers a passive event flag that unlocks optional NPC dialogue across multiple zones, specifically from characters who recognize what the ring is. What it is, is not explained unless those conversations are found.
Lore
Item description: A promise you failed to fulfil. Maybe you can still try again. The ring has no crafting origin and no purchase source — it is found, each time in a different place depending on which character's playthrough is active. A promise made before the game begins. The person it was made to is not identified. The game trusts the player to carry the weight of not knowing and not puts it down.
Red Scarf
Head
DEF 1
Function
1 defense. Categorized as a head item because the game's equipment system requires it, but it does not function as a helmet in any conventional sense. Wearing it in the Orphanage ruins or the Home Village triggers a unique, brief animation that plays once per playthrough and cannot be triggered again.
Lore
Item description: Reminds you of a distant past. The Red Scarf and the Black Dress are the game's two memory items — objects with no combat value that function entirely as emotional prompts. The Scarf's distant past belongs to whoever is holding it. The game does not specify. It does not need to. What matters is that the character holds something familiar enough to hurt and does not put it down.
Swift Boots
Accessory
DEF 4SPD +1Type Accessory
Function
The only accessory providing a speed bonus. The SPD +1 modifier increases dodge window frames and sprint distance per stamina unit. Minimal defense as a secondary benefit. Essential for builds relying on Agility Augmentation and Escape skill interactions — the speed bonus stacks with both skill effects for a combined mobility profile unlike any other loadout.
Description
You feel faster running around.
Lore
The inventory description is written in second person — one of only a few items in the game where the text speaks directly to the wearer rather than describing the item. The boots are not remarkable in appearance. What is remarkable is how they feel. Some things are only legible from inside them. The game understands that certain knowledge is only available to the person currently running.
Black Dressed Doll
Accessory
Type Accessory
Function
No stats. Equipping it alongside the Black Dress triggers a suppressed memory cutscene that is otherwise inaccessible. Certain NPCs react differently when the player carries the doll — dialogue options open that do not appear otherwise. The doll occupies an accessory slot without providing any mechanical benefit, meaning its value is entirely in what it unlocks rather than what it does.
Description
There's a strange sense of familiarity when holding this doll.
Lore
The doll and the Black Dress belong to the same person, or to the same memory of a person — the game is deliberate about leaving this ambiguous. Familiarity is not the same as memory. The doll is familiar the way grief is familiar: not because you recognize the specific thing, but because some part of you already knows the shape of it. The character who carries the doll has not been given language for what it means to them. The doll carries it anyway.
Red Amulet
Accessory
DMG +3Type Accessory
Function
Offensive accessory providing a flat damage bonus to all attacks regardless of weapon type. Pairs with the Red Gem material — lore-consistent items that share a lineage. The +3 bonus applies before enemy resistances, making it more valuable against lightly armored targets where the full bonus translates cleanly. The only accessory providing pure offensive enhancement without defensive trade-offs.
Description
You feel slightly stronger wearing this.
Lore
The Red Gem material is described as containing the soul of a great warrior. The Red Amulet, presumably made from or related to a Red Gem, passes something of that forward. Whether what is passed is strength in any meaningful sense, or simply the belief in strength, the game does not differentiate. The result is the same. You feel slightly stronger is either a metaphor or a mechanic. In Elucidate, both are true at the same time.
Arm Guard
Accessory
DEF 13Mind Res +1Type Accessory
Function
The only accessory combining physical defense with Mind State resistance. DEF 13 as an accessory is unusually high — paired with a body and head slot armour, it contributes meaningfully to overall protection. The Mind Res +1 reduces MS accumulation rate in Caligo-influenced zones. Critical for sustained exploration of the Underground Laboratory and inner sanctum areas.
Description
You feel much more protected.
Lore
Protection that works on two levels simultaneously. The physical guard is obvious — the arm is covered, the arm is harder to damage. The Mind Resistance is less obvious: whatever material the Arm Guard is made from has a property that the Church's records classify under anomalous material behavior and then do not elaborate on. Anomalous. That word appears seventeen times in Church documents related to items that resist Caligo. They never define it. They never explain why they keep using it.
Wind Essence
Relic
Type Companion Relic
Function
Given by the Tribe Chief when Pluvia chooses to accompany the Mercenary. Not a standard equipment item — it cannot be equipped or discarded. It occupies a unique inventory slot labeled carried. Its presence affects Pluvia's Foresight accuracy and provides a passive MS resistance effect only when both the Mercenary and Pluvia are in the same zone.
Lore
The Tribe Chief's words: It is not a gift. It is a promise that we believe in what she is walking toward. The Wind Essence was Pluvia's from the moment she was born — the Tribe held it in trust. It was always hers to carry; they were simply the ones keeping it safe until she chose to leave. That she chose to leave is the whole of what the Essence represents: the moment belonging and purpose point in different directions, and someone moves toward purpose.
